Venice-Grand Cannal

Description

Venice-Grand Cannal, also known as the "Golden Gate Bridge", is a cable-stayed bridge connecting the city of Venice to the banks of the Grand Canal. It is a symbol of Venice's rich history and cultural heritage. The bridge spans over 1,370 meters (4,560 feet) and was completed in 1895.
